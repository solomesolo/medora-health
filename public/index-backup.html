Why adoption fails

Adoption breaks between intent and workflow.

Digital health products fail to scale in predictable ways. The problem isn't demand — it's the missing system that carries adoption from demo to daily use.

  • The gap between pilot and real usage has no clear owner. Ownership fragments across product, sales, ops, and clinical stakeholders—so adoption stalls quietly.
  • Onboarding is designed for demos instead of daily workflows. It works in a meeting, but fails inside day-to-day clinical and operational reality.
  • Clinical, operational, and procurement realities are underestimated. Approvals, training, compliance, and change management create hidden friction that pilots don't surface.
  • Proof remains anecdotal instead of systematic. Without a measurement plan, outcomes can't be defended—and conversions slow down.
  • Adoption failure is structural, not promotional. Marketing can't solve it. Sales can't force it. Systems must be engineered for sustained usage.
  • Marketing cannot solve this.
  • Sales cannot force this.
  • Adoption failure is structural.
